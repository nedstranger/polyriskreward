About the book

In 2024, my wife and I received the results of a DNA paternity test that showed I'm not our daughter's biological father. We'd been in a happily open marriage since 2020, and although we knew the risks, we didn't expect her to get pregnant with another man.

The book tells the story from start to finish in forensic detail, describing how our open marriage started, how polyamorous dating works in practice, why we did the test, how we responded to it, and what the future looks like, especially with the biological father.

About the author

I am a songwriter, storyteller, and unapologetic oversharer. After graduating from Cambridge with a law master's, I moved to London to seek my fortune as a musician, not realising that I did the law-music thing the wrong way round.

Nevertheless, with my bandmate and best friend from uni, Vedantha Kumar, I grew our indie-folk band, August and After, to millions of streams in countries across the world, with features on official Spotify playlists, BBC Radio, Kerrang!, The Independent, and Songwriting magazine.

In 2018, I met Anya, a polyamorous, Latvian-born painter, and together we moved around Europe before settling in Vienna. Here, I launched my solo music project, Ned Stranger, and explored the risks and rewards of open marriage, inadvertently living the story that formed the basis for this book.
